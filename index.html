Professor Wole Soyinka

Notable Timeline

  • Prof. Wole Soyinka was born on the 13th of July, 1934 into a Yoruba family in Abeokuta.
  • He attended Government College, Ibadan in 1954 after which he attended University College Ibadan and University of Leeds, England.
  • After studying in Nigeria and England, he worked at the Royal Court Theatre in London.
  • In 1967, during the Nigerian Civil War, he was arrested by the federal government of General Yakubu Gowon and put in solitary confinement for two years, for volunteering to be a non-government mediating actor.
  • Soyinka was awarded the Nobel Prize for Literature in 1986, becoming the first African laureate
  • In 2014, he revealed his battle with prostate cancer

Prof. Wole Soyinka has inspired a lot of people in Nigeria
